My name is Mirjam and I was born and raised in Switzerland. My father is Dutch so I grew up with both languages, Dutch and (Swiss)German. My parents always spoke English with eachother, so I learned to understand English when I was quite young. As my grandmother is Italian, I learned to speak Italian as well. Although it is not very good, it is good enough for holidays in Italy (which I really love). In school I learned French and as I like languages, it stuck with me over the past few years so I can still use it when needed.

I started an apprenticeship as a bookseller when I was 16. This means I worked 3-4 days a week and went to school 1-2 days a week. I worked at a company which had three branches: a publishing group, a distribution centre and 15 bookstores. I worked in every department there, which gave me a lot of interesting insights into the book industry. After three years, I finished my apprenticeship and started to work at the distribution centre and the publishing group full time. At a certain point I got the possibility to organise our visits to bookfairs. We went to the Frankfurt Bookfair and the Bookfair Leipzig. Preparing our stay there was an experience from which I learned a lot and was fun to do!

In 2014 I wanted something new and as I had never experienced the Netherlands as my home country, just as a place where I would spend my vacation, I decided to move there. I started my BA German Language and Culture in 2016 at Leiden University. I did my MA German Language and Linguistics in Leiden as well and finished in August 2020. During my BA and my MA I took some courses with Dr. Jan Hillgärtner, who also taught some courses in the BDMS programme, in which we would learn more about newspapers, book critics and manuscripts. I found those courses very interesting. In December 2019 I followed a 3-day seminar at Leiden University, organised by the Plantin Institute Antwerpen, with Dr. Goran Proot College Boekwetenschap. These two experiences made me realize that I want to work with books again, which is why I applied for the MA BDMS.
